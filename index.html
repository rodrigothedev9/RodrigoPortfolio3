About Me.

From social media strategy to hand-drawn print designs to leading a floor of 15+ agents.

I'm the person who gets handed the messy stuff and hands it back organized.
I've managed Facebook, Instagram, and LinkedIn for a client, and designed what I managed — book covers, Wix websites, slide decks, and branding — built to look intentional, not templated. I also help run a family apparel business, leading a small production team that still runs strong on the days I'm not there.

Not being needed every day isn't a gap — it's proof the systems hold up without me standing over them.

I started in customer service and was promoted to team lead within months, and learned that growing a business and growing people come down to the same thing: clear systems and steady attention. I also studied IT, so I understand the tools I use — not just which buttons to click.
I'm ready, and I learn fast.

Most people specialize in one part of that process; I've built my career doing several at once, because in practice those things were never really separate to begin with. Running a page well means understanding the brand behind it. Designing an asset well means understanding who it's actually for. Leading a team well means understanding the numbers they're being measured against. I never had the luxury of learning these in isolation, so I learned them together, and I think that's made me better at each one than I would have been otherwise.

I managed Facebook, Instagram, and LinkedIn for a client, reaching hyper-local audiences with a posting rhythm built around when those audiences were actually paying attention, not just a generic schedule. Alongside that, I designed what I managed — book covers published on Amazon KDP, Wix websites, slide decks, and team branding — all built to look intentional rather than templated, because I've never believed good design and good strategy should live in separate departments.

I also help run a family apparel business, leading a small heat-press and sewing team that still runs strong on the days I'm not physically there — and if I'm honest, that's the part of my work I'm proudest of. Not being needed every single day isn't a gap; it's proof the systems I built actually hold up without me standing over them.

Before any of that, I got my start in a customer service role and was promoted to team lead earlier than most, coaching a full floor of agents through the kind of day-to-day problems that don't show up in training manuals — how to de-escalate a frustrated customer, how to explain a policy without sounding scripted, how to make a new hire feel capable in their first week instead of overwhelmed. My team landed recognition for it more than once, but what stuck with me wasn't the recognition — it was realizing that growing a business and growing people rely on the exact same two things: clear systems and steady, unglamorous attention.

I also studied information technology and can read and write in a handful of programming languages, not because I plan to be a developer, but because it means I actually understand how the tools I rely on every day are put together under the hood, rather than just knowing which buttons to click.

Whatever I'm handed — a CRM that's half set up, a client account that's lost its momentum, a new hire who needs someone patient in their corner — I approach it the same way every time: learn it properly first, then make it better than I found it. I'm not chasing a single job title, because I don't think my range fits neatly inside one. I'm looking for a team that needs someone who can run the page, design the assets, lead the people, and still keep the math honest at the end of the month.
